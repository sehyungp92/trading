======================================================================
  REGIME PREDICTOR IMPROVEMENTS -- CRITICAL EVALUATION
  Evaluated: 2026-03-30
======================================================================

This evaluates whether the 5 improvements in regime-predictor-improvements.md
have been optimally achieved, and whether the model's value as a conditioning
signal for downstream stock, swing, and momentum strategies has notably improved.

NOTE ON COMPARABILITY: The "full_diagnostics_optimal.txt" (Sharpe 1.536) uses
DAILY rebalancing (5723 rebalances). The EMA-only diagnostics uses WEEKLY
rebalancing (1148). For fair comparison, this evaluation uses the optimizer's
Phase 4 final metrics, which are ALL weekly-evaluated.

======================================================================
  2. IMPROVEMENT-BY-IMPROVEMENT GOAL ASSESSMENT
======================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  IMP 1: POSTERIOR CALIBRATION (rated HIGHEST IMPACT)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  GOAL:     AvgP(dom) in [0.60, 0.95], entropy > 0.15, Sharpe >= 1.3
  RESULT:   AvgP(dom) = 0.990, entropy = 0.029, Sharpe = 1.442
  VERDICT:  ** MOSTLY FAILED **

  The temperature + Dirichlet approach (Full Calibration) was found in the
  previous session to permanently dilute allocations with ~23% suboptimal
  regime weights -- i.e. it was HARMFUL to allocation quality. This is a
  legitimate finding: forced softening of inherently high-conviction posteriors
  is counterproductive.

  The EMA-only fallback (alpha=0.8) provides minimal softening:
    - Min P(dom) = 0.800 (vs 1.000 before) -- only at transition points
    - 98% of weeks still have P(dom) > 0.95
    - blend_policy_portfolios() and confidence_fallback() remain near-no-ops
    - Normalized entropy = 0.021 (target was > 0.15)

  CONSEQUENCE: The core problem -- instantaneous allocation flips with zero
  transition warning -- is barely mitigated. The ~3-week EMA fade provides
  some smoothing at regime boundaries but no meaningful probabilistic blending.

  SHARPE CHECK: 1.442 >= 1.3 target -- PASSED.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  IMP 2: LABEL ANCHORING ACROSS REFITS (rated HIGH IMPACT)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  GOAL:     State mean vectors correlate >= 0.8 across consecutive refits
  RESULT:   NOT IMPLEMENTED
  VERDICT:  ** NOT DONE **

  label_continuity_weight was never added to the config or optimizer.
  Evidence of label instability persists in year-by-year data:
    - 2013: G=72% -> 2014: D=50% (abrupt identity shift at refit)
    - 2023: G=49% -> 2024: S=74% (same pattern)
    - 2008: D=74% -> 2009: G=51% (but this one is a genuine macro shift)

  Some of these may reflect real economic transitions, but without anchoring
  metrics there is no way to distinguish real shifts from refit artifacts.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  IMP 3: REGIME TRANSITION MOMENTUM (rated HIGH IMPACT)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  GOAL:     risk_momentum turns negative >= 2 weeks before major crises
  RESULT:   Signal exists but has WRONG SIGN during 3 of 4 crises
  VERDICT:  ** FAILED **

  Crisis period risk_momentum:
    GFC (2008-09 to 2009-03):      +0.267  (should be negative)
    Euro Crisis (2011-08 to 2011):  +0.471  (should be negative)
    COVID (2020-02 to 2020-04):     +1.128  (should be negative)
    Rate Hike (2022-01 to 2022-10): -0.191  (correct, but marginal)
    SVB (2023-03):                   0.000  (no signal)

  ROOT CAUSE: With AvgP(dom)=0.990, posteriors are still near-binary.
  The regime switch happens INSTANTANEOUSLY (one week), then the EMA
  creates a brief ~3-week smoothing period. By the time the defined
  crisis window starts, the model has ALREADY switched to defensive
  and risk_momentum is measuring the STABILIZATION (positive) not the
  INCOMING SHIFT (negative).

  This confirms the plan's own prerequisite: "Improvement 1 must be done
  first. Binary posteriors have zero gradient -- momentum is always 0."
  Since Imp 1 failed to achieve meaningful posterior softening, Imp 3
  is structurally incapable of providing advance warning.

  The signal IS working mechanically (delta of posteriors over 4 weeks)
  but its utility as an EARLY WARNING system is zero with binary posteriors.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  IMP 4: FEATURE ENGINEERING (rated MEDIUM-HIGH)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  GOAL:     VIX feature improves regime entropy and/or Sharpe vs baseline
  RESULT:   Framework implemented but NO new features accepted by optimizer
  VERDICT:  ** NO IMPACT **

  The auto-optimizer was given candidates including add_vix, add_realized_vol,
  add_trend_div, but none were selected. This means none produced positive
  delta in the composite scoring function. The existing 4-feature set
  (growth, inflation, slope, spread) remains the best configuration found.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
  IMP 5: WEIGHT SMOOTHING (rated MEDIUM)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  GOAL:     Week-over-week allocation weight variance reduced >= 30%
  RESULT:   weight_smoothing_alpha = 0.5 accepted by optimizer
  VERDICT:  ** LIKELY MET **

  With alpha=0.5 (50% new, 50% previous), week-over-week weight changes
  are mechanically dampened. Current stability metrics:
    w_SPY: avg_abs_change=0.0233
    w_TLT: avg_abs_change=0.0168
    w_GLD: avg_abs_change=0.0285
    w_CASH: avg_abs_change=0.0323

  Without pre-smoothing baseline for exact comparison, the ~50% dampening
  factor from alpha=0.5 likely exceeds the 30% reduction target.

  BONUS: Composite score improved from Full Cal (0.671) to EMA-Only (0.682)
  partly because weight smoothing + stability_weight=0.8 jointly improved
  the scoring function's stability components.

======================================================================
  5. VALUE AS DOWNSTREAM CONDITIONING SIGNAL
======================================================================

  The regime predictor was intended to provide conditioning signals for
  stock, swing, and momentum families to set portfolio-level configs,
  risk allocation, sizing, and priority rules.

  WHAT THE MODEL CAN RELIABLY PROVIDE TODAY:
  -------------------------------------------

  a) CATEGORICAL REGIME LABEL (G/R/S/D)
     - Changes ~2x per year (47 transitions over 22 years)
     - Stable within regime (avg spell ~116 weeks in daily, ~24 weeks)
     - Per-regime allocation profiles are economically sensible:
         G (22%): SPY-heavy, moderate cash      (growth/recovery)
         R (19%): SPY+EFA, some GLD             (reflation/expansion)
         S (25%): GLD+CASH heavy, low equity    (stress/inflation hedge)
         D (34%): TLT+CASH dominant, minimal eq (defensive/deleveraging)
     - USEFUL FOR: Binary risk-on/risk-off decisions, broad allocation tilts

  b) CRISIS OVERLAY (p_crisis)
     - Correctly spikes during GFC (0.967), COVID (0.877), 2022 (0.910)
     - Active >50%: 35.3% of weeks (overly broad? was 21.7% in optimal)
     - THE MOST VALUABLE SIGNAL for real-time risk management
     - USEFUL FOR: Emergency leverage reduction, position sizing caps

  c) LEVERAGE SIGNAL (L)
     - Range: 0.048 to 1.600 (with L_max=1.6)
     - Avg: 1.026, meaningfully varies by regime and crisis state
     - Responds well to crisis overlay (L=0.434 during GFC, 0.316 Euro)
     - USEFUL FOR: Direct leverage/sizing multiplier for strategies

  d) ALLOCATION WEIGHTS (w_SPY, w_EFA, w_TLT, w_GLD, w_CASH, w_IBIT)
     - Smoothed (alpha=0.5) for signal stability
     - Weekly avg_abs_change ~2-3% per asset
     - USEFUL FOR: Portfolio-level asset allocation guidance

  WHAT THE MODEL CANNOT PROVIDE (despite being planned):
  ------------------------------------------------------

  a) GRADUAL TRANSITION SIGNALS
     - Posteriors are still 99% binary -> no gradient for continuous adjustment
     - A strategy wanting to "gradually reduce equity exposure as we approach
       a defensive regime" gets NO advance signal -- it sees 100% G one week
       and 100% D the next
     - blend_policy_portfolios() and confidence_fallback() remain no-ops

  b) ADVANCE WARNING OF REGIME SHIFTS
     - risk_momentum has wrong sign during crises (measures stabilization,
       not incoming shift)
     - No 2-4 week lead time as intended
     - Downstream strategies get ZERO warning before regime flips

  c) PROBABILITY-WEIGHTED BLENDING
     - With P(dom) ~= 1.0, there is no meaningful way to blend regime-specific
       configs proportionally
     - Strategies must use binary if/else on dominant regime, not weighted mix

  d) CONFIDENCE-BASED CAUTION
     - Confidence avg = 0.970, rarely drops below 0.9
     - No ability to signal "low conviction, be cautious" periods
     - confidence_fallback() essentially never triggers


======================================================================
  6. REGIME CLASSIFICATION ACCURACY (for what it's worth)
======================================================================

  The plan says "label accuracy is not the concern" but allocation signal
  quality IS. The regime labels determine allocation weights, so misclassified
  periods get suboptimal allocations. Key concerns from critical_evaluation.txt:

  MISMATCHES (from optimal version, similar pattern in EMA-only):
    - 2003-2007 pre-GFC bull: classified D (88%), expected G
      -> Model held TLT+CASH during equity bull -> missed returns
      -> 2003-2007 returns: +11.2%, +2.5%, +2.6%, +0.4%, +6.5%
         vs 60/40:          +17.6%, +10.2%, +6.6%, +9.7%, +7.8%
      -> Underperformed 60/40 by cumulative -28.7% over 5 years

    - 2013-2019 expansion: too much S (32%), should be more G
      -> Some equity underweight during expansion
      -> But still outperformed: +17.8%, +11.1%, -2.1%, +9.8%, +11.8%,
         +2.1%, +23.8% (mixed vs 60/40)

    - 2020 COVID: classified S/R, expected D
      -> Allocation stayed more risk-on during crash
      -> But only -2.8% max drawdown (vs 60/40 at -17.8%) due to crisis overlay
      -> CRISIS OVERLAY compensated for regime misclassification

    - 2022 rate hikes: classified D (71%), expected S
      -> Held TLT during rate hikes (wrong!) but cash + low leverage saved it
      -> Only -4.6% return (vs 60/40 at -22.8%)
      -> Again, CRISIS OVERLAY + LEVERAGE did the heavy lifting

  INSIGHT: The crisis overlay (p_crisis) and leverage governor are doing most
  of the risk management work. The regime labels themselves are often wrong,
  but the PORTFOLIO-LEVEL controls (crisis deleverage, sigma floor, vol cap)
  compensate. This means the regime labels' direct conditioning value is
  LIMITED -- the real value is in the crisis + leverage signals.


======================================================================
  7. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT: HAS THE MODEL'S DOWNSTREAM VALUE IMPROVED?
======================================================================

  HONEST ANSWER: MARGINALLY.

  The improvements plan identified 4 key structural weaknesses:
    1. Binary posteriors -> allocation flips are instantaneous        UNRESOLVED
    2. Label instability across refits                               UNRESOLVED
    3. No transition momentum signal                                 INEFFECTIVE
    4. Untested OOS robustness                                       UNTESTED

  What DID improve (but from Phase 3-4 tuning, not from the 5 improvements):
    - MaxDD: 12.75% -> 8.72% (excellent drawdown control)
    - CAGR: 8.80% -> 11.11% (strong return improvement)
    - Calmar: 0.690 -> 1.275 (dramatically better risk-adjusted return)

  These gains come from crisis_weights, delta_rho_threshold, L_max, sigma_floor,
  stability_weight, weight_smoothing -- standard portfolio/leverage tuning, NOT
  from posterior calibration, label anchoring, or momentum signals.

  FOR DOWNSTREAM STRATEGIES, the practical improvement is:
    - BETTER drawdown control -> strategies can trust the leverage signal more
    - SMOOTHER weights -> less noisy allocation signal
    - SAME binary regime switching -> no new gradual-adjustment capability
    - SAME lack of advance warning -> no new early detection capability

  The model remains a GOOD binary risk-on/risk-off switch with strong crisis
  response, but it has NOT become the nuanced conditioning signal envisioned
  in the improvements plan.


======================================================================
  8. RECOMMENDATIONS
======================================================================

  A. ACCEPT THE EMA-ONLY MODEL AS THE CURRENT BEST
     - Highest composite score (0.682), best drawdown control (8.72%)
     - Marginal Sharpe difference vs Full Cal (-2.5%) offset by much
       better tail risk (MaxDD -18.2%)

  B. ABANDON POSTERIOR CALIBRATION VIA TEMPERATURE/DIRICHLET
     - The HMM's well-separated emission means produce inherently binary
       posteriors. Post-hoc softening dilutes allocations without adding
       information. This is a fundamental architectural limitation.

  C. FOR MEANINGFUL POSTERIOR UNCERTAINTY, CONSIDER ARCHITECTURAL CHANGES:
     - Bayesian HMM (MCMC/VI) that outputs true posterior uncertainty
     - Ensemble of HMMs with different initializations (disagreement = uncertainty)
     - Mixture-of-experts approach where multiple models vote
     - These are fundamentally different from the current hmmlearn approach

  D. PRIORITIZE WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS:
     - The CRISIS OVERLAY (p_crisis) is the most valuable signal -- invest
       in making it more responsive and accurate
     - The LEVERAGE GOVERNOR effectively manages risk -- refine its parameters
     - The WEIGHT SMOOTHING improves signal quality -- keep alpha=0.5
     - Stop trying to extract gradual transitions from a binary classifier

  E. RUN WALK-FORWARD VALIDATION
     - The 4th structural weakness (untested OOS robustness) remains
     - Run: python -m research.backtests.regime.cli walk-forward --test-years 3
     - This is the MOST IMPORTANT remaining validation step
     - If OOS/IS ratio < 0.60, the in-sample metrics above are unreliable

  F. FOR DOWNSTREAM STRATEGY CONDITIONING, USE:
     - Dominant regime label (G/R/S/D) for broad allocation tier selection
     - p_crisis for real-time risk scaling (the model's strongest signal)
     - Leverage (L) as a direct sizing multiplier
     - DO NOT rely on risk_momentum, posterior entropy, or confidence
       (all near-degenerate with binary posteriors)
     - Consider adding a simple 4-week rolling volatility signal OUTSIDE
       the HMM as a more reliable transition indicator
